What is the Juwai Teer Result?

The Juwai Teer Result represents the outcome of a traditional archery game played in the Jaintia Hills district of Meghalaya. While Shillong and Khanapara Teer are often more widely discussed in mainstream media, Juwai Teer holds a special place in the hearts of the locals in Ladrymbai and the surrounding Jaintia region. It is a daily event that combines indigenous sporting culture with the excitement of numerical prediction.

Every day, excluding Sundays, archers gather to test their skills, and thousands of enthusiasts wait eagerly for the official Juwai Teer Result. At Teer Daily, we recognize the importance of this event and strive to provide the most accurate numbers directly from the ground.

How is the Juwai Teer Result Calculated?

The mechanism behind the Juwai Teer Result is consistent with other Teer games in Meghalaya, ensuring transparency and trust. The result is not generated by a computer but is the product of human skill and physical arrows.

  • The Event: Archers from various local clubs gather at the designated ground in the Jaintia Hills.
  • The Shooting: In the First Round, archers shoot 30 arrows each. In the Second Round, they shoot 20 arrows each.
  • The Count: After the shooting stops, officials publicly count the arrows that hit the target.
  • The Result: The final Juwai Teer Result is determined by taking the last two digits of the total number of arrows hitting the target.
Example: If 682 arrows hit the target in the first round, the FR result is declared as 82.

FR and SR: Understanding the Timings

The game plays out in two distinct sessions, known as the First Round (FR) and the Second Round (SR). Understanding the timing is crucial for anyone tracking the Juwai Teer Result.

While timings can sometimes vary based on the specific association's schedule (sometimes Juwai is played earlier in the afternoon), the standard tracking format follows the afternoon schedule. It is vital to check the specific board for live updates. Our portal updates the numbers the moment the officials declare them on the ground.

The anticipation builds up from the morning, with people discussing "Common Numbers" and dream interpretations, culminating in the declaration of the FR and SR numbers.

The Cultural Significance in Meghalaya

Juwai Teer is not just a game; it is a livelihood for many. From the archers who hone their skills daily to the small counters that operate across the district, the ecosystem of Teer supports the local economy. It is regulated under the Meghalaya Amusements and Betting Tax Act, which ensures that the sport remains legal and organized.

The game also keeps the tradition of Khasi and Jaintia archery alive. In a world dominating by digital sports, Teer remains a grounded, physical tradition that connects the modern generation with their ancestral heritage.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Is Juwai Teer Result available on Sundays?

No, the archery associations in Meghalaya observe Sunday as a weekly holiday. No games are played, and no results are declared on Sundays.

2. Where is Juwai Teer played?

Juwai Teer is primarily played in the Jaintia Hills district of Meghalaya. It is a distinct event from the Shillong and Khanapara games.

3. What are "Common Numbers"?

Common numbers are statistical estimates based on previous results and dream interpretations. They are not official results but are used by enthusiasts for analysis.

4. Why are the timings sometimes different for Juwai?

Juwai and Ladrymbai Teer games sometimes operate on a morning/early afternoon schedule (e.g., 1:30 PM). However, standard reporting slots often align with the broader Teer schedule. Always check the live status on our board.

5. How can I verify the result?

The most reliable way is to check multiple credible websites or visit the local counters in Meghalaya where official sheets are distributed.

6. Can I participate online?

No, official Teer participation is an offline activity conducted at licensed counters within Meghalaya. Online websites only provide result information.

7. Is Juwai Teer different from Ladrymbai Teer?

They are often grouped together as they both originate from the Jaintia Hills region, but they can be separate events depending on the specific archery club organizing the day's shoot.

8. What happens if the result is delayed?

Delays can happen due to weather conditions (heavy rain) or administrative reasons. In such cases, the "Status" on our scoreboard will remain as "Awaited" until the official declaration.
