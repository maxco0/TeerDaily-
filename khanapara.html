In the vibrant landscapes of Northeast India, the Khanapara Teer Result holds a significance that goes far beyond a simple game of numbers. Every day, thousands of people from Guwahati, Shillong, and the surrounding districts of Ri-Bhoi wait with anticipation for the outcome of this traditional archery sport. What is Khanapara Teer?

Khanapara Teer is a traditional archery game organized by the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute. It takes place in Themmarwet, a small village in the Ri-Bhoi district of Meghalaya, located very close to the Assam border (Khanapara). Because of its proximity to Guwahati, it is popularly known as "Khanapara Teer" and has a massive following in Assam.

Note: While many confuse it with Shillong Teer, the Khanapara Teer Result is a distinct event with its own set of archers, timings, and results, although the rules of the game remain largely similar.

How is the Khanapara Teer Result Calculated?

The transparency of the result generation is what maintains the trust of the people. The process is physical, visible, and strictly regulated.

The Shooting Process:
Every afternoon (except Sundays), a group of skilled archers gathers at the shooting ground. They sit in a semi-circle and shoot arrows at a cylindrical target made of cane or bamboo, placed roughly 50 meters away.

The Counting:
Once the shooting stops, officials collect the arrows that have successfully hit the target. The counting is done publicly. The final Khanapara Teer Result is derived from the last two digits of the total number of arrows that hit the target.

For example, if 1,248 arrows hit the target in the first round, the result for that round is 48. If 785 arrows hit in the second round, the result is 85.

Decoding FR and SR: Timing and Structure

When you look for the Khanapara Teer Result, you will always see two distinct numbers. These correspond to the two sessions played daily:

  • FR (First Round): This is the major round involving more arrows and archers. The counting process concludes, and the result is usually declared between 3:45 PM and 4:15 PM IST.
  • SR (Second Round): This is the subsequent round, which happens shortly after. The result for this round is typically available around 4:45 PM IST.

The Cultural Significance

Teer is not merely a game; it is an institution in Meghalaya. It provides employment to archers, ticket sellers, and administrative staff. It preserves the ancient Khasi tradition of archery, adapting it to the modern world. When you check the results, you are witnessing the outcome of a sport that has survived for centuries.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Is the Khanapara Teer Result the same as the Assam Teer Result?

Yes, because the game is played on the border of Meghalaya and Assam (Khanapara), it is often referred to locally as the Assam Teer Result, even though the ground is technically in Meghalaya.

2. Why is there no result on Sundays?

The archery association observes Sunday as a weekly holiday. Additionally, the game is closed on national holidays and specific church holidays.

3. What are "Common Numbers"?

Common numbers are mathematical projections or estimates calculated by analysts based on previous results. They are not official results but are popular among enthusiasts for analysis.

4. Can the result be three digits?

No. The result is always a two-digit number ranging from 00 to 99. If the total arrow count ends in 00 (e.g., 600), the result is declared as 100 in some contexts, or simply 00.

5. Is this website official?

No, Teer Daily is an informational news portal. We are not affiliated with the government of Meghalaya or the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute. We simply report the public results.

6. How do I know if the result is correct?

We recommend cross-referencing results from multiple reliable sources. However, we strive to maintain 100% accuracy by sourcing data directly from the venue.

7. What is a "Teer Dream Number"?

In local culture, it is believed that dreams can predict the outcome of the Teer result. Specific objects seen in dreams (like water, fire, or animals) are associated with specific numbers.

8. Are the results legal?

Teer is a legal, regulated sport within the state of Meghalaya under the Meghalaya Amusements and Betting Tax Act. Users from other states should be aware of their own local laws regarding such activities.
