Understanding Teer Previous Results and Historical Data

For enthusiasts and observers of the traditional archery games in Meghalaya, keeping track of Teer Previous Results is an essential part of the experience. Unlike many other games of chance, the Teer games—specifically Khanapara, Shillong, and Juwai—are deeply rooted in the cultural history of the region. The results are not generated by machines but by the physical skills of archers from the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute and other associated bodies. This page serves as a comprehensive digital archive, providing a clean, accessible Teer 1 Month Result History for informational and analytical purposes.

The Importance of a 1 Month Teer Result History

Accessing a consolidated 1-month history allows users to view the ebb and flow of the game's outcomes. Many followers of the sport believe in analyzing patterns, although the game remains mathematically random. By providing a clear table of the last 30 days, we ensure transparency and easy reference. Whether you missed a result from last Tuesday or are simply cross-referencing data, having the Teer 1 Month Result History on a single page saves time and ensures accuracy.

Khanapara Teer Previous Results: An Overview

Khanapara Teer Previous Results are among the most searched data points in the region. Located near the border of Meghalaya and Assam, the Khanapara range attracts significant attention. The game operates under strict regulations, and the results are declared in two rounds daily (except Sundays and holidays). The First Round (FR) usually concludes around 3:45 PM, followed by the Second Round (SR) at 4:45 PM.

Our archive ensures that even if you miss the live announcement, the data is preserved here. The Khanapara chart is often utilized by enthusiasts to check for "common numbers" or repetition, although it is important to remember that every day is a fresh event with independent outcomes.

Shillong Teer Previous Results: The Cultural Core

The Shillong Teer is perhaps the most famous of the three. Organized at the Polo Ground in Shillong, it is a game that involves 50 archers shooting 30 arrows each in the first round and 20 arrows in the second. The Shillong Teer Previous Results recorded on this page reflect the official count of arrows hitting the target. The winning number is determined by the last two digits of the total number of arrows hitting the target.

For example, if 745 arrows hit the target, the result is 45. This transparent, physical method of determining results is what makes the historical data so fascinating to compile. Our 1-month table captures these outcomes accurately, providing a reliable resource for verifying past games.

Juwai Teer Previous Results: Morning and Afternoon

While Khanapara and Shillong are evening events, Juwai Teer often operates on a slightly different schedule, often referred to as the morning or early afternoon teer. Juwai Teer Previous Results are vital for those who follow the earlier games of the day. The mechanics remain similar—archers, targets, and arrow counts—but the timing differs. The tables above distinguish Juwai results clearly, ensuring there is no confusion between the different locations.

Understanding FR and SR Timings

In all our previous result tables, you will see columns for 'FR' and 'SR'.

  • FR (First Round): This is the major round with the highest number of arrows shot. It typically occurs between 3:45 PM and 4:15 PM depending on the location.
  • SR (Second Round): This is the subsequent round with fewer arrows, occurring roughly an hour after the first.

The combination of these two rounds makes up the daily record. In our archives, if a day is marked "Off," it indicates a Sunday or a scheduled holiday where no archery took place.

Data Transparency and Online Portals

In the digital age, reliance on word-of-mouth for results has diminished. Online portals play a crucial role in maintaining Teer Old Results. We prioritize speed and accuracy. However, maintaining a historical archive is equally important. It serves as a logbook for the community. We ensure that our tables are mobile-friendly so that users in remote areas with basic smartphones can still access the Teer 1 Month Result History without heavy data usage.

Responsible Awareness

It is imperative to view these results as informational data. The game of Teer is regulated by the Meghalaya Amusements and Betting Tax (Amendment) Act 1982. While it is legal and regulated in Meghalaya, users from other regions should be aware of their local laws. This page provides historical data for educational and informational purposes only and does not facilitate participation in the games.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Teer Previous Results?

Teer Previous Results are the archival records of winning numbers from past dates for archery games conducted in Meghalaya, used for historical reference.

Where can I find Khanapara Teer Previous Results for the last month?

You can find the complete 1-month record for Khanapara Teer on this page, listed in a chronological table format.

Are these previous results official?

These results are sourced from the respective archery grounds. However, we act as an information aggregator. Always verify with official counters for absolute certainty.

Why is Sunday marked as 'Off' in the result history?

Most Teer associations, including Shillong and Khanapara, do not conduct games on Sundays. Hence, the result is marked as 'Off' or 'Holiday'.

What is the difference between FR and SR in the result history?

FR stands for First Round and SR stands for Second Round. The game is played twice daily, and results are recorded separately for each round.

How far back does the Teer 1 Month Result History go?

Our 1 Month Result History displays data from the current date back to exactly 30 days prior, providing a comprehensive recent snapshot.

Is Juwai Teer result history included here?

Yes, a dedicated section for Juwai Teer Previous Results is included on this page with its own 1-month data table.

Do previous results guarantee future outcomes?

No. Teer results are based on the random outcome of archery shots. Previous results are for informational and archival purposes only and do not influence future games.
