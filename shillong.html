Understanding the Shillong Teer Result

The Shillong Teer Result is more than just a daily number; it is the heartbeat of Meghalaya's traditional sporting culture. Organized by the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute (KHASI), this event draws attention not only from the locals in Shillong but from enthusiasts across Northeast India. At Teer Daily, we are dedicated to providing you with the most accurate, timely, and official updates straight from the Polo Ground.

Every afternoon, except on Sundays, the air in Shillong fills with anticipation as the archery session begins. Whether you are a local resident checking the "House" and "Ending" numbers or a visitor intrigued by this unique tradition, understanding the mechanics of the Shillong Teer Result is essential to appreciating the sport.

How Shillong Teer Works

Unlike modern lotteries governed by random number generators, the Shillong Teer Result is physical, transparent, and grounded in skill. The process involves two rounds of archery shooting.

  • The Archers: A group of 50 archers from different clubs within the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute participates.
  • The Shooting: In the First Round, each archer shoots 30 arrows. In the Second Round, they shoot 20 arrows.
  • The Calculation: The arrows that hit the cylindrical target are counted publicly. The final result is determined by the last two digits of the total number of arrows.
Example: If 1,574 arrows hit the target in the first round, the Shillong Teer Result for FR is declared as 74.

FR and SR: The Two Daily Milestones

When tracking the Shillong Teer Result, you will always find two sets of numbers declared daily. These correspond to the two sessions:

  1. First Round (FR): This is the primary event. The shooting usually begins around 3:30 PM, and after the counting process, the result is officially declared at 3:45 PM IST.
  2. Second Round (SR): Following a short break, the second session commences. The result for this round is typically announced at 4:45 PM IST.

Accuracy in reporting these times is crucial. Our team at Teer Daily ensures that the numbers displayed on our scoreboard match the official announcement from the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute.

The History and Cultural Significance

The origins of Teer can be traced back to the early 20th century. Archery has always been an integral part of the Khasi culture, used historically for hunting and defense. Over time, it evolved into a competitive sport among local clans.

In the early 1980s, recognizing the popularity of betting on these archery outcomes, the Government of Meghalaya regulated the sport under the Meghalaya Amusements and Betting Tax Act. This move transformed Teer into a legal, regulated activity that generates revenue for the state and supports the livelihoods of thousands of archers and administrative staff.

Why People Search for Shillong Teer Result Daily

For many in Meghalaya, the Shillong Teer Result is a daily ritual. It is not just about the potential financial gain but the thrill of prediction. The culture of "Teer Dreams" is unique to this region, where people interpret their dreams to predict the winning numbers. Seeing a snake, water, or fire in a dream translates to specific digits, adding a layer of mysticism to the mathematical probability.

Transparency and Trust

One of the reasons for the enduring popularity of the Shillong Teer Result is the transparency of the system. The counting of arrows is done in an open ground where representatives and public spectators can witness the process. This ensures that the result is fair and free from manipulation.

Responsible Awareness and Legality

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Is the Shillong Teer Result declared on Sundays?

No, the Khasi Hills Archery Sports Institute observes Sunday as a weekly holiday. No games are played, and no results are declared. The same applies to major national holidays.

2. What is a "Common Number"?

A "Common Number" is a predicted number calculated by analysts based on previous results and mathematical formulas. It is not an official result but a statistical estimate.

3. Where is the official counting held?

The official shooting and counting of arrows take place at the Polo Ground in Shillong, Meghalaya.

4. Can the result be a triple-digit number?

No, the result is always a two-digit number ranging from 00 to 99. If the total arrow count ends in 00 (e.g., 800), the result is 00 (sometimes referred to as 100).

5. How accurate are the results on this website?

We strive for 100% accuracy by sourcing our data directly from the venue. However, we always recommend cross-referencing with official boards.

6. What is the difference between Shillong and Khanapara Teer?

While the rules are similar, they are two separate events held at different locations. Shillong Teer is held in Shillong, while Khanapara Teer is held in the Ri-Bhoi district near the Assam border.

7. Are there evening or night rounds?

Yes, recently, "Night Teer" rounds have been introduced, but the primary and most popular results remain the afternoon FR and SR rounds mentioned on this page.

8. Is this an official government website?

No, Teer Daily is an independent news portal. We are not affiliated with the Government of Meghalaya or the Archery Association.
